About N3C

N3C stands for the No-Code Coding Club — a community created to help students from all backgrounds learn how to build real, functioning digital tools without needing to write a single line of code.

About the Founder – Ken Matsuura

My name is Ken Matsuura. I am a fourth-year business student at Capilano University with a concentration in International Business and Logistics. I’m also the creator of N3C.

I started N3C because I noticed something missing — a space for students who think creatively, but don’t have a technical background. With no-code tools like Notion, AI, Zapier, and Webflow, anyone can bring big ideas to life. This club gives students from any program the tools and support to actually build things — quickly.

Interests & Hobbies

I like to experiment. I’m into building automation systems, developing tools using AI. I also enjoy training, reflecting, and traveling — anything that pushes me physically or mentally.

My Goal with This Club

The dream is to create a launchpad. I want N3C to give people the confidence and skills to start their own projects — fast. Workshops, hackathons, community builds — whatever gets ideas out of heads and into the world. Eventually, I want this to be bigger than CapU
