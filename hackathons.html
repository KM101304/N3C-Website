Hackathons @ N3C

N3C Hackathons are open-invitation, time-limited events where creators of all backgrounds work in small teams to build real, no-code products. These aren't just design jams — they're focused on delivering MVPs using modern tools and rapid collaboration.

Goals of Our Hackathons

Our events aim to:

  • Enable non-technical students to build functional products quickly
  • Encourage cross-discipline collaboration
  • Showcase what can be built using tools like Webflow, Zapier, Figma, and AI
  • Develop execution and iteration skills under pressure

How It Works

Participants form teams at the start of the event. A challenge theme is announced, and then teams use no-code tools to create their solution within a 24-hour window. Mentors provide support. Final projects are presented to a panel of judges.

Featured Hackathon Story

How Team Starling Built an Award-Winning Mental Health App in 24 Hours
By Megan Slack • June 27, 2023 • CreativeBloq.com

At the 2023 MindHack London event, a student team called Starling created a mental health tracking app designed for young people. Built using no-code platforms like Glide and Canva, the app impressed judges with its polished UI and focus on user empathy. What set Starling apart was their ability to rapidly wireframe, test, and iterate on a working solution — all within one day. Their story is a testament to how meaningful, functional apps can be launched without writing a single line of code.
