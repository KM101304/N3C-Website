What is a Hackathon?

A hackathon is an intense, time-boxed event where individuals or teams collaborate to build a solution — whether it's an app, automation, prototype, or tool. At N3C, our hackathons are designed to help non-coders build real, functioning projects using no-code platforms.

Goals of Our Hackathons

  • Empower students from all backgrounds to create solutions fast
  • Foster collaboration between creative and technical minds
  • Encourage experimentation and problem-solving under pressure
  • Launch working MVPs in under 48 hours

How It Works

Hackathons at CapU typically run for 24 to 48 hours. Participants form teams, pick a challenge, and race to create a working solution using tools like Webflow, Airtable, Zapier, Framer, and AI builders. We bring in mentors, snacks, workshops, and good vibes. Everyone walks away with something real — a prototype, a pitch, or a polished workflow.

Why You Should Join

Even if you’ve never built anything before, N3C hackathons are your chance to start. You’ll get hands-on experience, meet new collaborators, and leave with a project you can show off. It’s less about winning and more about doing.

Stories From Other Hackathons

Last semester, a group of first-time hackers built a job application funnel using Notion and Make.com. Another team launched a mental wellness chatbot using Tally Forms, OpenAI, and Webflow — in just one day. These weren’t coders — just students with ideas, motivation, and support.
